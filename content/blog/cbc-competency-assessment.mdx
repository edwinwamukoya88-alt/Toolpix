## Understanding the CBC Assessment Framework

Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) represents a fundamental shift from the traditional 8-4-4 system. Instead of focusing on memorization and exams, CBC emphasizes the development of competencies — practical skills and knowledge that learners can apply in real-life situations.

The Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) has designed the CBC framework around seven core competencies:

1. **Communication and Collaboration**
2. **Critical Thinking and Problem Solving**
3. **Creativity and Imagination**
4. **Citizenship**
5. **Digital Literacy**
6. **Learning to Learn**
7. **Self-Efficacy**

## Assessment Levels in CBC

CBC assessment is continuous and happens at three main levels:

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

This happens daily in the classroom. Teachers observe learners, ask questions, and review work to gauge understanding. Formative assessment helps teachers adjust their teaching in real-time.

### Summative Assessment (End of Term)

At the end of each term, teachers evaluate learners against expected outcomes. This includes projects, practical tasks, and written assessments.

### National Assessments (Key Milestones)

At specific grades, learners sit for national assessments:

- **Grade 3**: Kenya Early Years Assessment (KEYA)
- **Grade 6**: Kenya Primary School Education Assessment (KPSEA)
- **Grade 9**: Kenya Junior School Education Assessment (KJSEA)
- **Grade 12**: Kenya Senior School Education Assessment (KSSEA)

## The Four Competency Levels

CBC uses a four-level rating system for each competency:

| Level | Code | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| Exceeding Expectations | EE | Learner consistently performs above expected standard |
| Meeting Expectations | ME | Learner meets the expected standard consistently |
| Approaching Expectations | AE | Learner is making progress but not yet at expected standard |
| Below Expectations | BE | Learner requires significant support to meet the standard |

## How Teachers Record Assessments

Teachers track learner progress using several tools:

- **Observation schedules** for noting skills and behaviors
- **Portfolios** collecting learner work over time
- **Checklists** for tracking competency mastery
- **Rubrics** with clear criteria for each performance level

## Reporting to Parents

CBC report cards differ significantly from the 8-4-4 system. Instead of just letter grades, CBC reports include:

- **Competency ratings** for each learning area
- **Teacher comments** on learner progress
- **Areas of strength** and areas needing improvement
- **Suggestions for parental support**

## Conclusion

CBC assessment is designed to be more holistic than traditional systems. By focusing on competencies rather than exam scores, it provides a richer picture of learner development. The key to successful CBC implementation is using the right tools and maintaining consistent assessment practices throughout the term.
