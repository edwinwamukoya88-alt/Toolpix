## Understanding the KICD Lesson Plan Format

The Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) requires CBC lesson plans to include specific components. A properly structured lesson plan ensures that teaching aligns with curriculum expectations and that learning outcomes are measurable.

### Essential Components of a KICD Lesson Plan

A standard KICD-compliant lesson plan includes:

1. **Learning Area** - The subject being taught
2. **Grade Level** - Specific grade (1-12)
3. **Strand & Sub-Strand** - The topic within the learning area
4. **Lesson Number** - Sequential numbering within a strand
5. **Learning Outcomes** - What learners should achieve by lesson end
6. **Core Competencies** - Which of the 7 core competencies are developed
7. **PCIs** - Pertinent and Contemporary Issues
8. **Learning Resources** - Materials needed for the lesson
9. **Lesson Development** - Step-by-step teaching process
10. **Assessment Methods** - How learning will be evaluated
11. **Reflection** - Teacher notes after the lesson

## Step-by-Step Guide to Using a Lesson Plan Generator

### Step 1: Set Your Curriculum Context

Enter your specific details:
- Grade level and learning area
- Term and week number
- School and teacher name

### Step 2: Select Strand and Sub-Strand

Choose from KICD curriculum strands or enter custom topics. A good generator includes an auto-complete feature for common strands.

### Step 3: Define Learning Outcomes

Write clear, measurable outcomes using action verbs aligned to Bloom's Taxonomy. The generator can suggest appropriate verbs for each cognitive level.

### Step 4: Add Core Competencies and PCIs

Select which of the 7 core competencies your lesson develops and any relevant PCIs (e.g., Life Skills, Environmental Education, ICT integration).

### Step 5: Structure Lesson Development

The generator provides a structured template:
- **Introduction** (5 min): Engage learners through questions or activities
- **Lesson Development** (30 min): Step-by-step learning activities
- **Conclusion** (5 min): Summary and assessment

### Step 6: Generate and Download

With one click, generate a formatted lesson plan ready for printing or digital use.
