## What is a CBC Grade Calculator?

A CBC Grade Calculator is a specialized tool designed for Kenyan teachers implementing the Competency-Based Curriculum. It automates the calculation of learner scores across multiple assessment areas and converts raw scores into the standard CBC competency levels.

## Why Use a Digital Calculator?

Manual grade calculation in CBC is time-consuming because:

- Each learning area has multiple strands and sub-strands
- Assessments include projects, practicals, and observations
- Scores must be tallied across weeks and terms
- Teachers manage 40+ learners per class
- Multiple learning areas need individual tracking

A digital calculator eliminates errors and saves hours of work each week.

## Key Features of a Good Calculator

When choosing a CBC grade calculator, look for:

### 1. Multi-Assessment Support

The calculator should handle different assessment types:
- Written tests and exams
- Practical projects
- Oral assessments
- Observation checklists
- Portfolio evaluations

### 2. Automatic Level Determination

Based on weighted average scores, the tool should automatically assign:
- **EE (Exceeding Expectations)**: 80-100%
- **ME (Meeting Expectations)**: 60-79%
- **AE (Approaching Expectations)**: 40-59%
- **BE (Below Expectations)**: 0-39%

### 3. Term and Year Tracking

Good calculators let you track progress across:
- Single assessments
- Term totals
- Yearly summaries

### 4. Report Generation

The ability to generate formatted reports for parent-teacher conferences saves significant time.

## Tips for Accurate CBC Assessment

To get the most out of your grade calculator:

- **Use consistent scoring rubrics** across all assessments
- **Weight assessments appropriately** — projects may count more than daily work
- **Update scores regularly** rather than waiting until end of term
- **Double-check data entry** before generating reports
